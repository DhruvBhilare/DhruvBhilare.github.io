Auto Room Lights

The Idea

This project was inspired by my dad’s frustration with my habit of forgetting to turn off the lights when leaving my room. While looking into automatic light switch solutions, I noticed a major flaw in traditional motion-sensor-based systems—they often turn off even when someone is still inside but not moving. To address this, I designed a more reliable system that accurately detects occupancy and ensures the lights remain on as long as someone is in the room.

The device is installed at the room’s entrance and uses high-precision, long-distance IR sensors to count the number of people entering and leaving. The lights automatically turn on when someone enters and stay on until the last person leaves, eliminating unnecessary energy usage. Additionally, I integrated a Bluetooth-controlled app and a manual override switch for flexible control, allowing the lights to be operated wirelessly or manually as needed.

How It Works

  • The system uses IR sensors at the doorway to track the number of people in the room.
  • If count is greater than 0: The lights remain on.
  • If count is less than 1: The lights turn off.
  • A Bluetooth app allows wireless control, overriding the occupancy-based mechanism.
  • A traditional switch on the device provides a manual override option.

    • This approach solves the common issues with motion-sensor-based lighting while providing added convenience through multiple control options.
