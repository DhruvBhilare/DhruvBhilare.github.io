AirMo-V1.0

The Idea

AirMo is my latest creation: a wireless, gesture-based, wearable computer mouse designed to revolutionize the way we interact with computers. Traditional computer mice are medically proven to strain your wrists over time. AirMo offers an innovative solution to this problem, aiming to provide a more ergonomic and versatile input device for computer users.

How It Works

AirMo utilizes an accelerometer and gyroscope to track hand movements, seamlessly translating them into mouse cursor control. Equipped with two triggers on its underside, AirMo enables essential functionalities like right-clicking, left-clicking, and even gesture-based scrolling.

What sets AirMo apart is its reprogrammable nature. Users can customize it to execute tasks such as adjusting volume, copying, cutting, pasting, or any other action they need, all through gestures and button combinations. As a wireless device, AirMo provides unparalleled freedom of movement, allowing users to interact with their computers from virtually anywhere within range.

What's Next

Currently in its first prototype phase, AirMo is an ongoing project I'm actively developing throughout the semester. Future plans include:

  • Refining the gesture recognition algorithms for improved accuracy
  • Expanding the range of customizable actions
  • Improving battery life and power efficiency
  • Developing a user-friendly interface for easy customization
  • Exploring potential applications in virtual and augmented reality

    • Stay tuned for updates as I continue to push the boundaries of wearable technology with AirMo!
